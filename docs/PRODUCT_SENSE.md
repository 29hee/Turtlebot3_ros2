# Product Sense

## Problem

In a museum, a visitor with limited mobility cannot easily move to a specific exhibit
or find it among many. They need help reaching the exhibit and seeing its numbered
panel clearly.

## Target Users

- Primary: museum visitors with limited mobility who want guided access to a chosen
  exhibit.
- Secondary: museum staff who set up the environment (map, panel numbers) and operate
  the robot.

## Value Proposition

The robot turns "I want to see exhibit 7" into autonomous, predictable guidance: it
leads to the exhibit and positions itself so the panel is clearly visible from the
front — accessibility without requiring a human escort for every visit.

## Non-Goals

- Not a general tour-narration or voice-assistant product.
- Not multi-robot fleet coordination.
- Not dynamic re-mapping while actively guiding a visitor.

## Success Metrics

- Recognition accuracy of panel numbers during scanning.
- Share of selected numbers where the robot arrives and achieves front-facing
  alignment within tolerance.
- Time from number selection to front-facing arrival.
- Verified operation on the physical TurtleBot3 Burger.
